Project - General Practice via Remote Electronic Proxy (GP-REP)

Overview

The title for my assignment project is General Practice via Remote Electronic Proxy, or GP-REP for short. It is a system of compact aerial drones that act as both a communication device for a doctor and a delivery mechanism of tests for patients in remote communities to have access to regular healthcare from a doctor if there are no medical practitioners in the area. As an example, a patient in a rural town where the closest doctor is a city more than 400km away may not be able to have a check-up due to job constraints or the nature of their illness, an appointment can be made with a doctor using a drone to administer diagnosis by instructions from the doctor a general diagnosis kit the drone carries.

Motivation

While the concept of medical delivery via drones is not new, the drones currently in use are designed more for specific procedures and collecting samples for lab tests, usually with the doctor already on scene for equipment use or back in the lab to run tests. My project idea centres around using a drone system for live, non-emergency check-ups to maintain one’s health for those who can’t attend an appointment in person. Over the past few years, due to a global pandemic, those stuck inside have missed regular appointments for the sake of safety while risking undetected problems. Using virtual conferences to substitute physical appointments means that the consultation won’t have the benefit of diagnostic tests that can be done in person. The proposed drone system should as an online clinic to give consultation without losing as much utility.

Description

The GP-REP would be comprised of two primary systems. The first is the medical appointment system; to create and store medical records of patients for their designated practitioner, book appointments and assign what kind appointment it would be (i.e., short or long, diagnostic or routine check-up, etc). This is a simple doctors’ network, not unlike the current secretarial structure for clinics and hospitals. A key difference would be the entry for an appointment address, as it will determine several statistical queries needed for the drone system to work such as the distance from the nearest drone launch site and the feasibility of a proxy check-up if the address is not in a suitable environment.

The second system pertains to the aerial drones, as they are the foundation of what makes this project idea achievable. The drones must be equipped several information technology devices for practical medical use, such as a communication system consisting of cameras and microphones to facilitate doctor-patient contact, navigation software and sensors to determine flight path, inventory cataloguing to check manifests of medicinal items that will be delivered and/or brought back and environment sensors to take data of the area of the appointment to decide whether or not it is clean enough to do a test.

A medical proxy appointment would be carried out thusly; first a booking would be made online on a website representing the network of doctors who agreed to participate in the GP-REP system. The patient will fill out the usual medical appointment details with the addition of the address where the medical exam would be intended to take place. The address entry would be compared to the database of general practice clinics and return a list of available doctors sorted by closest distance first. Assuming a patient decides on which doctor they will make an appointment with and the date and time, the details are saved in both the clinic’s booking system and the drones’ system.

The drones can be launched from either directly from the clinic of the chosen doctor or a storage facility dedicated to maintaining and equipping the drones for medical purposes. When the appointment time nears the drone’s information system will notify staff of appropriate adjustments to be made to the drone for the proxy consultation, whether it would be specific medical tests to be loaded onto the drone’s carry-on compartment or adjusting the drone’s blades if the weather at the time is predicted to have strong winds.

GPS navigation will then direct the drone to the designated address and the appointment will take place after it arrives and the patient takes it to a private setting. Camera and microphone communication will allow the consultation between the doctor and patient to take place and instructions can be given verbally on how to use any test kits delivered or medical measuring equipment the drone has attached. Once the appointment has concluded the call, test kit data and any other relevant information is stored on the system before the drone is directed back to its place of origin and stored for reuse.

Tools and Technologies

Various mark-up languages can be used to write the online registration website to facilitate the booking system, since Intro into Information Technology teaches HTML in its first few courses it would be an ideal start to write up the website if it. For the storage of patient and doctor information the programs required can be written in Python and stored via the Pandas modules. While there are drones that can deliver medicine via parachute such as the Zipline™ drones, the required drones actually land and be transported for online consultation. Such hardware would require a capability to lift a few kilograms for both test kits and equipment. Amazon Prime Air’s model of drone has not only this weight bearing capability, but also inbuilt GPS guidance.

Skills Required

Programming language writing skills – Python, HTML
GPS software experience
Drone maintenance skills

Python programming would require several months of tutoring and practice to obtain the level of skill to create a sufficient data storage system as well a GPS navigation for the drones. The drone hardware will be harder to obtain the skills to maintain as it will require specialist hardware knowledge of not only drones but also aerial transportation, training and acquisition of the drones will be expensive.

Outcome

GP-REP could expand the reach of regular healthcare to rural town areas and those who are separated due to lockdowns or covid isolations. Even after the pandemic ends it will allow online consultations with doctors to be more productive with the introduction of mobile medical tests instead of video chat only. Accessibility to online services increase consumer traffic across all services, even in medicine; thus GP-REP would be a huge improvement in distributing healthcare for a wider audience. Improvements made to mobile medicinal practices can increase diagnosis rates and clinic capabilities.
